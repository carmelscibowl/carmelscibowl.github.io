Join Carmel Science Bowl

Open to all Carmel High School students. No prior Science Bowl experience required.

What We Do

Carmel Science Bowl is a competitive academic team that prepares throughout the school year for two main events: the Indiana Regional Science Bowl in February and, for the A team, the National Science Bowl in late April.

We field three teams — A, B, and C — selected through a fall tryout process. All three compete at Regionals; the A team also travels to MIT Science Bowl (TBD) in November and Nationals in Washington, D.C. in the spring.

Tryout Process

Tryouts run for roughly 9 weeks each fall, starting in late August. Students compete in two rooms: an advanced room (mostly returners and upperclassmen, eligible for A Team selection) and a novice room (mostly new students, ineligible for A Team selection).

How to Prepare

  • Pick one or two subjects you actually enjoy and study them deeply. A specialist who consistently hits their subject is more valuable than a generalist who buzzes on nothing. Science Bowl tests on much the same syllabus as the national olympiads, which quickly gets intractable for someone who only learns "stock."
  • Practice buzzing. The fastest way to improve buzzing is by having someone read for you and racing to identify the answer before the question ends. Alternatively, you can practice on a number of buzzing sites such as speriphery.
  • Just show up. Tryouts are friendly. Most members are happy to help newcomers learn the format. A Team members are happy to share advice for self-studying and more.

Starter Resources

  • Math: AoPS Intro series, past AMC problems.
  • Physics: Khan Academy AP Physics 1, Morin's Problems and Solutions in Introductory Mechanics, past F=ma exams.
  • Chemistry: Atkins Chemical Principles, past USNCO exams.
  • Biology: Campbell's Biology, chapters 1-5 to start.
  • Earth & Space: Tarbuck's Earth Science, Karttunen's Fundamental Astronomy.
